    About Me

    My name is Shannon Miller and I'm a recent graduate from the School of Visual Arts with a BFA in film and a concentration in screenwriting. I've been making my own short films since I was thirteen years old and have always had a strong passion for storytelling. I love writing, animating, and crafting my own stopmotion puppets. My paper animated short Rose and Tulip was awarded Best College Animation by the Count Basie Film festival, and my live action short Haunting Heather was screened at the Garden State Film Festival. Having grown up reading series such as the Chronicles of Narnia, Maximum Ride, and Cinder, as well as watching stopmotion animated classics such as James and the Giant Peach and Coraline, fantasy has always been a major source of inspiration for me. My goal as an artist is to transport my audience into a world of whimsy and imagination while also inviting them to confront issues that exist in a real-life modern world.

    About Phoenix

    Phoenix is a stopmotion fantasy short film about a teenage witch named Mai. Having grown up in a religious rural town and strict family, Mai has always been taught that witchcraft was the worst evil of all. When the beloved family cow, Daisy, falls ill and passes away, Mai can't help but intervene, even if it runs the risk of exposing her secret and getting her disowned. This short film will act as a trailer for a feature-length screenplay I have been writing and editing for the past two years. The goal of this short is help get my story noticed, so that Phoenix the feature film is eventually picked up and put into production. With this story, I'm interested in posing a question: Is it worth weighing your true identity and sense of freedom against your relationship with your loved ones and acceptance from society as a whole? And what does it really mean to belong?
